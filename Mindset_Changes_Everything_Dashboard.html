Fixed vs. Growth Mindset

ðŸ”’

Fixed Mindset

The less helpful one

Core Belief
"Intelligence and skills are inherent traits. You either have them, or you don't."

Your Job: PROVE yourself

  • 1

    Look Smart

    Intense desire to prove you're right, all the time, about everything

  • 2

    Avoid Challenges

    "Stay in your box with your cards" - only do what you know you can nail

  • 3

    Give Up Easily

    "Quitting is way better than failing" - quitting is ME deciding

  • 4

    Disdain Effort

    "Effort is for people who aren't good at stuff" - struggle = no card

  • 5

    Fear Feedback

    Terrified of hearing "you're not enough" - and that can't be fixed

  • 6

    Resent Others

    Schadenfreude - if you have more, I have less (scarcity mindset)

Outcome:

Plateau at a certain point. "So busy proving how awesome I already am, I never find out how awesome I actually could be."

Growth Mindset

The more helpful one

Core Belief
"Intelligence and skills are largely the result of learning and effort."

Your Job: IMPROVE yourself

  • 1

    Desire to Learn

    Do whatever it takes to get better - accelerate progress toward your limit

  • 2

    "I can do anything until proven otherwise" - that's an empirical question

Continuous improvement. "Fail their way right on past the fixed mindset plateau."

The Three Levers of Mindset Shift

1
Education
"Probably the #1 thing you can do"

You've got to teach people mindset. This is the #1 thing you can do to shift somebody's mindset.

Critical Point:

"Mindset is not something where you can sneak up behind somebody's back and change their mindset without them knowing. It doesn't work that way."

For Resistant (Fixed Mindset) People:

  • The Reverse Ask: "Can I tell you about fixed/growth, and then YOU tell ME where you see ME struggle with fixed mindset?" (They're happy to tell you what you're bad at)
  • The Shared Care Object: Focus on something you both care about (company, team, child) - "How do we create growth for THIS thing we both care about?"
2
Feedback
+39.1% performance impact

Note: Feedback includes BOTH praise AND criticism. Everything here applies to both, at work and at home.

Giving Feedback Better: 4 Principles

1. Ask for Permission

"Is now good, or would later work better?" For leaders, it's really about timing - let them get a Snickers first if needed.

2. Say It How THEY Can Hear It

Like "love languages" but for feedback. Learn their preferences.

3. Focus on Behavior

Never about the person - always about behavior. Behaviors can change.

4. Just Do It

Don't delay. As close to the behavior as possible (after cooling off if needed).
